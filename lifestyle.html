Tea Ceremony

Kyoto was where the tea ceremony was born and remains its spiritual heart. Tea has been drunk in Japan for over 1,000 years, but it was only around the 16th century that the practice took on the highly ritualized form by which it is know today. Warlords, wealthy merchants and literati all competed to lay on the most lavish and sophisticated tea ceremonies.

Temples

Visiting temples and shrines is a must do when traveling in Kyoto. There are over 1500 of them in Kyoto.

Heritage

Kyoto is home to numerous UNESCO World Heritage Sites. You’ve probably already seen a picture of the famous “Zen” garden here, with its mystical 15 rocks floating in a sea of white gravel.

Snow

It snows in Kyoto several times each winter, usually between early December and mid-March. Most of the time, the snow doesn’t stick at all, but two to four times a winter, it stays on the ground, though it almost always melts within a few hours. But it is always beautiful

Geisha

Geisha (芸者 geisha) are a class of female Japanese performance artists and entertainers trained in traditional Japanese performing arts styles. Their distinct appearance is characterised by long, trailing kimono, traditional hairstyles and oshiroi make-up

Cherry Blossom

The blossoms, (桜の花, Sakura no hana) in Japanese, last only for a few days, but their appearance is tremendously important, both economically and culturally. Friends and family get together, and Instagram is awash with pictures.

Night Sky

Kyoto's beauty is further accentuated by the untainted night sky

Kyoto Samurai

150 years ago, Kyoto was a key battleground for Japan’s samurai, a noble class of warriors divided by loyalty to clan and country Learn More

Fushimi Inari Shrine

Fushimi Inari Shrine (伏見稲荷大社, Fushimi Inari Taisha) is an important Shinto shrine in southern Kyoto. It is famous for its thousands of vermilion torii gates, which straddle a network of trails behind its main buildings. The trails lead into the wooded forest of the sacred Mount Inari, which stands at 233 meters and belongs to the shrine grounds.

Attractions

There's more to the allure of Kyoto than just its historical treasures. You can also enjoy its diverse and stunning scenery: exquisite valleys whose appearance changes with each of the four seasons, lush green hillsides, beautiful seascapes, vibrant green tea fields, and traditional rural townscapes. Discover all of Kyoto's spectacular scenery that should be seen at least once in your life.
